First — my story 👋

How I actually landed my remote job

Here's the part people assume is the "secret": I did a Master's in AI in Germany, then worked at Capgemini in Cologne for three years. On paper I had the degree, the international experience, the big-name company. The "perfect" CV.

Then I moved back to Pakistan — and I hit a wall.

I spent a whole year searching. A year of applying, waiting, refreshing my inbox, hearing nothing back. Sit with that: even with a foreign Master's and three years at a global firm, the "apply and wait" method didn't work. Not for a month. For a year.

So I stopped waiting. I found a remote role on LinkedIn and applied through the posting first — but this time I didn't stop there. I found the CEO of the company and messaged them directly. I didn't beg. I introduced myself, told them exactly what I'd done, showed them my actual work, and told them why I could help their company specifically.

They replied. And what turned that reply into an offer wasn't my degree — it was the conversation. I kept the communication genuine, human, and consistent with the CEO. That back-and-forth built the trust. That's what got me hired.

That one message turned "one application in the pile" into a real relationship — and that relationship turned into the remote job that changed my life. The one that lets me work from home, a café, or the beach, as long as I've got good Wi-Fi. 🌊💻

Since then I've done it again and again. And I'm going to show you exactly how, step by step. This isn't theory — it's the exact system I used.

A perfect CV got me a year of silence. One direct message to the CEO got me the job. Credentials open the door — but reaching out walks you through it.

1

Turn your LinkedIn into a magnet 🧲

Before you apply anywhere, fix this. Recruiters search LinkedIn for people. Set up right, jobs come to you.

Your headline is a billboard, not a job title

❌ Software Engineer

✅ ML/AI Engineer | Python · LLMs · RAG · MLOps | Shipping AI features that cut cost & latency | Open to Remote

Put "Open to Remote" in the headline text itself — many recruiters filter by keywords right there, not just the green frame.

Set up your profile to rank

  • Turn on "Open to Work" — set to Recruiters only if you don't want your current boss to see
  • Pin your 3 strongest skills to the top; add 10–20 relevant ones (not 50 random)
  • Take LinkedIn skill assessments — verified badges rank you ~30% higher in recruiter search
  • Mirror the exact words from job posts you want ("remote," "async," "Python," "LLM," "RAG," "AWS," "MLOps")
  • Post once a week — even 3 sentences. Active profiles show higher in search, even to passive recruiters

2

Where to actually find remote jobs 🌍

Stop scrolling random Facebook groups. These are the legit, remote-first boards in 2026:

Best all-rounders

  • We Work Remotely — huge remote community
  • Working Nomads — global, daily digests
  • Remote OK — tech & startups, great filters
  • JustRemote — curated global roles

Higher-paying / curated

  • Himalayas — best-designed, salary upfront
  • Remote100K — vetted $100k+ roles
  • FlexJobs — paid, but scam-free

Startups (fast hiring)

  • Wellfound — founders + AI/ML startups
  • YC "Work at a Startup" — tons of AI hires
  • Dynamite Jobs — 100% remote-first only

AI / tech-specific 🤖

  • ai-jobs.net — AI/ML/data, salary-transparent
  • Hugging Face Jobs — labs + open-source AI
  • HN "Who is Hiring?" — founders post directly

Pro tips

  • Set email alerts on 3–4 boards
  • Apply within 24–48h — early gets seen
  • Use multiple boards to widen the net

🚩 Scam check: if they ask for money, want bank details "for equipment," or only interview over WhatsApp with no company footprint — walk away. Real remote jobs never charge you.

3

Apply so you actually get noticed 📩

Your application is a 6-second pitch. Make it count:

  • Tailor the top of your CV to each role — match the title + 3–4 key phrases from the post (AI screeners look for these)
  • One page. Results, not duties — "Cut inference latency 40% & API cost 3× with a distilled LLM," not "responsible for machine learning"
  • Short cover note, not an essay — who you are, one win, why this company, that you'd love to talk
  • Have a portfolio link — a GitHub with 2–3 real projects, Hugging Face, Kaggle, or a short demo/Loom beats "references on request." For AI/dev roles, shipped code they can click is the strongest signal

But most people stop here. That's exactly why they don't get hired. The next move is the one that changed everything for me. 👇

🔥 The Secret Weapon
4

Message the decision-maker directly

Here's how I actually landed jobs:

After I applied, I didn't wait. I found the CEO or hiring manager on LinkedIn — and messaged them directly. I told them who I was, showed them my work, and told them exactly why I'd be great for the role.

That's the move 95% of people are too nervous to make. It's why my application jumped from "one of 300" to "the one they remember." A thoughtful, specific message from a real person who did their homework stands out instantly. You're no longer a line in a spreadsheet — you're a human they've already talked to.

How to do it, step by step

  • Apply first (normally, through the posting). The DM is the follow-up, not a replacement.
  • Find the right person: startup → the Founder/CEO; bigger company → the Hiring Manager or Head of [your department]
  • Send a short, specific, human message — never "please give me a job"
The message that works Hi [Name], I just applied for the [Role] position and wanted to reach out directly because I'm genuinely excited about what [Company] is building — [one specific, real detail: a product, a launch, a post they wrote].

A quick bit about me: I [one relevant win — "built a RAG chatbot that cut support tickets 30%," "shipped an ML pipeline handling 1M+ requests/day," "fine-tuned an LLM for X"]. A couple of examples / my GitHub here: [link].

I'd love to show how I could help with [specific thing the role needs]. Even 10 minutes would mean a lot. Thank you! 🙏

The rules that make it work

✅
Reference something specific and real about them or the company — this one thing multiplies your reply rate
✅
Keep it short — 5 seconds to read; an essay gets scrolled past
✅
Show, don't beg — lead with a result + link, not "I really need this job"
✅
Follow up once, gently, after 3–5 days — then let it go
❌
Don't copy-paste the same DM to 50 people — personalize the first line every time

Do this for every job you apply to. Applied to 10? Send 10 messages. This one habit out-performs 100 blind applications.

Bonus 🎯

Make LinkedIn hunt for you

The best remote jobs are filled before they're posted — through referrals. Get on that list:

  • Follow the companies you want — you'll see openings first
  • Engage genuinely with posts from people who work there — a thoughtful comment beats a cold DM
  • Ask for referrals right: "Hey [Name], I'm applying for [Role] at [Company] — would you be open to referring me or sharing tips?" Referred candidates get interviewed far more often
  • Grow to 500+ connections — more reach = more inbound
Is it worth it? 💳

LinkedIn Premium — buy it or skip it?

Short answer: it can be worth it — but only for a focused sprint, not as a forever subscription. Here's the honest breakdown so you don't waste money.

What actually helps for job hunting

  • InMail credits — the big one. Message people you're not connected to (that CEO or hiring manager) without waiting for a connection request. For the "message the decision-maker" move, this feature pays for itself
  • "Who viewed your profile" — see which recruiters are checking you out, so you can follow up while you're fresh in their mind
  • How you rank vs. other applicants — tells you if you're competitive before you waste time on a role
  • Company & salary insights — headcount trends, who's hiring, salary ranges — so you target right and don't lowball yourself
  • LinkedIn Learning — free courses + certificates you can add to your profile

🎯 The honest truth

✅
You do NOT need Premium to get hired. Everything in this guide — including DMing the CEO — works on a free account (you can DM connections, and requests to founders are often accepted). Premium just makes outreach faster and higher-volume
✅
Worth it when you're in active hunting mode, sending lots of cold InMails to decision-makers every week — that's when the credits and insights earn their keep
❌
Not worth it when you're casually browsing or applying to 1–2 jobs a week and not using the InMail credits — you'll pay monthly for features you don't touch

💡 My recommendation

  • Start free. Optimize your profile, apply, and send connection requests with a note to the people you want to reach
  • Then use the free 1-month trial during one intense hunting month — fire off applications and direct messages daily, and squeeze every InMail credit out of it
  • Cancel before it renews unless it's clearly landing you conversations. Set a reminder the day you start the trial
Premium is a speed boost, not a requirement. A free account plus the courage to reach out beats Premium plus sitting quietly. Don't buy the tool if you won't do the work.
