## MOVE 1 — Turn your LinkedIn into a magnet 🧲

Before you apply anywhere, fix this. Recruiters *search* LinkedIn for people. If your profile is set up right, jobs come to **you**.

### ✅ Your headline is a billboard, not a job title
Don't write "Software Engineer." Write what you do + your stack + your value:

> ❌ *Software Engineer*
> ✅ *ML/AI Engineer | Python · LLMs · RAG · MLOps | Shipping AI features that cut cost & latency | Open to Remote*

**Put "Open to Remote" or "Open to Work" in the headline text itself** — many recruiters filter by keywords right in the headline, not just the green frame.

### ✅ Turn on "Open to Work" — the smart way
- Employed and don't want your boss to see? → set it to **"Recruiters only."**
- Not worried? → use the green **#OpenToWork** frame. It genuinely gets you more messages.

### ✅ Load up on the right keywords
LinkedIn's 2026 search is AI-powered — it ranks you on *relevance*, not just exact matches. So:
- Pin your **3 strongest skills** to the top.
- Add **10–20 relevant skills** (not 50 random ones).
- Take LinkedIn **skill assessments** — verified skill badges rank you ~30% higher in recruiter searches.
- Mirror the exact words from job posts you want ("remote," "async," "Python," "PyTorch," "LLM," "RAG," "AWS," "MLOps," etc.).

### ✅ Post once a week — even 3 sentences
LinkedIn now shows *active* profiles higher in search, even to recruiters browsing passively. One short post a week ("here's something I learned about X this week") keeps you visible. You don't need to go viral. You need to exist.

## MOVE 2 — Where to actually find remote jobs 🌍

Stop scrolling random Facebook groups. These are the boards that are legit and remote-first in 2026:

**Best all-rounders**
- **We Work Remotely** — one of the biggest remote communities (tech, marketing, support, design)
- **Working Nomads** — global roles, daily email digests
- **Remote OK** — tech & startup roles, great filters
- **JustRemote** — curated global roles across industries

**Higher-paying / curated**
- **Himalayas** — best-designed board of 2026, shows salary + culture upfront
- **Remote100K** — hand-vetted roles paying $100k+
- **FlexJobs** — paid, but screens out the scams (worth it if you keep hitting fakes)

**Startups (where founders hire fast)**
- **Wellfound** (formerly AngelList) — direct access to founders and hiring teams; huge for AI/ML startup roles
- **Dynamite Jobs** — 100% remote-first employers only
- **Y Combinator's "Work at a Startup"** — YC-backed companies, tons of AI startups hiring engineers directly

**AI / tech-specific (target these if you're technical)**
- **ai-jobs.net** — dedicated AI/ML/data roles, remote filter, salary-transparent
- **Hugging Face Jobs** — AI/ML roles at labs and startups building in the open
- **Cerebral Valley / AI job boards** — early-stage AI companies moving fast
- **Hacker News "Who is Hiring?"** — the monthly thread; founders post directly, replies get read fast

**Pro tips:**
- Set up **email alerts** on 3–4 boards so new jobs come to you.
- **Apply within 24–48 hours** of a posting. Early applicants get seen; week-old ones get buried.
- 🚩 **Scam check:** if they ask for money, want your bank details "for equipment," or interview only over text/WhatsApp with no company footprint — walk away. Real remote jobs never charge you.

## MOVE 3 — Apply so you actually get noticed 📩

Your application is a 6-second pitch. Make those seconds count.

- **Tailor the top of your CV** to each role. Match the job title and 3–4 key phrases from the post. AI screeners look for these.
- **One page. Results, not duties.** "Cut model inference latency 40% and API cost 3x by moving to a distilled LLM," not "responsible for machine learning."
- **Short cover note, not an essay.** 4–5 lines: who you are, one relevant win, why *this* company, and that you'd love to talk.
- **Have a portfolio/link.** A **GitHub** with 2–3 real projects, a Hugging Face profile, a Kaggle, or a short demo/Loom of something you built beats "references available on request." For AI/dev roles, *shipped code the person can click* is the single strongest signal.

But here's the thing — most people *stop here*. And that's exactly why they don't get hired.

The next move is the one that changed everything for me. 👇

## MOVE 4 — 🔥 THE SECRET WEAPON: Message the decision-maker directly

Here's the honest truth about how I actually landed jobs:

> **After I applied, I didn't wait. I found the CEO or hiring manager on LinkedIn — and I messaged them directly. I told them who I was, showed them my work, and told them exactly why I'd be great for the role.**

That's it. That's the move 95% of people are too nervous to make. And it's the reason my application jumped from "one of 300" to "the one they remember."

Think about it: the founder of a small company checks their LinkedIn. A thoughtful, specific message from a real person who *did their homework* stands out instantly. You're no longer a line in a spreadsheet — you're a human they've already talked to.

### How to do it (step by step)

**1. Apply first (normally, through the posting).** The DM is the follow-up, not a replacement.

**2. Find the right person on LinkedIn.** Search the company + a title:
- Small company / startup → the **Founder / CEO**
- Bigger company → the **Hiring Manager**, **Head of [your department]**, or a **recruiter**

**3. Send a short, specific, human message.** NOT "please give me a job." Do this instead:

> **Template — the message that works:**
>
> *Hi [Name], I just applied for the [Role] position and wanted to reach out directly because I'm genuinely excited about what [Company] is building — [one specific, real detail: a product, a recent launch, a post they wrote].*
>
> *A quick bit about me: I [one relevant win — "built a RAG chatbot that cut support tickets 30%," "shipped an ML pipeline handling 1M+ requests/day," "fine-tuned an LLM for X"]. A couple of examples / my GitHub here: [link].*
>
> *I'd love the chance to show how I could help with [specific thing the role needs]. Even 10 minutes of your time would mean a lot. Thank you! 🙏*

### The rules that make it work
- ✅ **Reference something specific and real** about them or the company. This one thing multiplies your reply rate.
- ✅ **Keep it short.** 5 seconds to read. If it's an essay, they scroll past.
- ✅ **Show, don't beg.** Lead with a result and a link, not "I really need this job."
- ✅ **Follow up ONCE**, gently, after 3–5 days. Then let it go.
- ❌ Don't copy-paste the same DM to 50 people. They can tell. Personalize the first line every time.

**Do this for every single job you apply to.** Applied to 10 jobs? Send 10 direct messages. This one habit will out-perform sending 100 blind applications.

## BONUS — Make LinkedIn hunt for you (the referral game) 🎯

The best remote jobs are filled *before* they're ever posted — through referrals. Here's how to get on that list:

- **Follow the companies you want.** You'll see their openings first.
- **Engage genuinely** with posts from people who work there. A thoughtful comment gets you noticed faster than a cold DM.
- **Ask for referrals the right way.** Find someone at the company, build a tiny bit of rapport, then: *"Hey [Name], I'm applying for [Role] at [Company] — I really admire the team. Would you be open to referring me or sharing any tips?"* Referred candidates get interviewed at a dramatically higher rate.
- **Grow your network to 500+.** More connections = you show up in more searches = more inbound.

## Is LinkedIn Premium worth it? 💳

Short answer: **it can be — but only for a focused sprint, not as a forever subscription.** Here's the honest breakdown so you don't waste money.

### What actually helps for job hunting
- **InMail credits** — this is the big one. Premium lets you message people you're *not* connected to (like that CEO or hiring manager) without waiting for a connection request to get accepted. For the "message the decision-maker" move, this is the feature that pays for itself.
- **"Who viewed your profile"** — see which recruiters and companies are checking you out, so you can follow up while you're fresh in their mind.
- **See how you rank vs. other applicants** — tells you if your profile is competitive for a role *before* you waste time on it.
- **Company & salary insights** — headcount trends, who's hiring, salary ranges. Useful for targeting the right companies and not lowballing yourself.
- **LinkedIn Learning courses** — free with Premium; genuine bonus if you want a certificate to add to your profile.

### The honest truth 🎯
- **You do NOT need Premium to get hired.** Everything in this guide — optimizing your profile, applying early, and even DMing the CEO — works on a **free account** (you can DM anyone you're connected to, and connection requests to founders are often accepted). Premium just makes the outreach *faster and higher-volume*.
- **Where it's worth it:** you're in *active hunting mode*, sending lots of cold InMails to decision-makers every week. That's exactly when the InMail credits and insights earn their keep.
- **Where it's NOT worth it:** you're casually browsing, applying to 1–2 jobs a week, or not using the InMail credits. You'll pay every month for features you're not touching.

### My recommendation 💡
1. **Start free.** Optimize your profile, apply, and send connection requests with a note to the people you want to reach. Get a feel for it.
2. **Then use the free 1-month trial** during one intense hunting month — the month you're firing off applications *and* direct messages daily. Squeeze every InMail credit out of it.
3. **Cancel before it renews** unless it's clearly landing you conversations. Set a reminder the day you start the trial.

> Premium is a *speed boost*, not a *requirement*. The free account plus the courage to reach out beats Premium plus sitting quietly. Don't buy the tool if you're not going to do the work.
